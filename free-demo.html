DSCR plain English: Debt Service Coverage Ratio. Maria earns $22,000/month and spends $17,500. That leaves $4,500. If she takes a $25K loan at $513/mo, her DSCR = $4,500 ÷ $513 = 8.77. That's excellent — but lenders also look at reserves and trends, not just DSCR.
Minimum lenders require: DSCR = 1.25 (you earn $1.25 for every $1 owed). Maria is well above this with no current debt, which is why income-based metrics are strong.

EMI = Equated Monthly Installment — the fixed payment you make every month on a loan. Like a car loan payment.

It combines principal repayment + interest into one flat number. The formula uses your loan amount, interest rate, and term to calculate a payment that pays off the loan completely by the end date.

Example: Maria borrows $25,000 at 8.5% over 60 months. Each month she pays $513 — about $300 goes to principal, $213 to interest at first. As principal shrinks, more goes to principal over time.
Extend the term to lower monthly payments:
  • 3-year term: $25K at 8.5% = $789/mo (total paid: $28,404)
  • 5-year term: $25K at 8.5% = $513/mo (total paid: $30,800)
  • 7-year term: $25K at 8.5% = $391/mo (total paid: $32,844)
Extending from 3yr to 5yr lowers payment by 35% ($789 ? $513) at a cost of only $2,396 extra interest over the life of the loan. That trade-off is usually worth it for cash flow.

Break-even is the minimum revenue you need to cover all costs. Below this = guaranteed losses. Above this = potential profit.

Simple example: If Maria's rent + utilities + insurance = $8,000/month, she must earn at least $8,000 before she makes a single dollar of profit. But because ingredients and packaging cost 45 cents per dollar of sales, she actually needs to sell $14,545 worth of food just to cover costs.

The safety margin is how far above break-even you are. A 34% margin means Maria's revenue could drop 34% before she starts losing money. In the food business, 20–35% margin is considered healthy.
Two levers: lower fixed costs OR reduce variable cost ratio

  • Reduce variable costs: Negotiate bulk ingredient pricing (saves 2–5% COGS)
  • Cut fixed costs: Review "Other $3,300" — often has 10–15% waste
  • Raise revenue: 1 extra catering gig/month at $1,500 improves margin from 34% to 40%
  • Drop low-margin items: Some menu items cost 60–65% to make — removing them raises overall variable margin
Goal: Push safety margin to 40%+ before taking the loan.
