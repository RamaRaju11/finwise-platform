EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) = a fixed amount you pay every month that covers both principal (the loan amount) and interest.

Formula: EMI = P — r — (1+r)n — ((1+r)n - 1), where P = loan amount, r = monthly rate, n = months.

Plain English: You borrow $20K. The bank charges 8.5%/year. You pay $413/month for 60 months. Total: $24,780. The extra $4,780 is the bank's fee for lending you money.
●
Rule of thumb: Keep your total monthly debt payments (all loans combined) under 30% of your net income. David's $413 is 5.9% — he could theoretically take on $1,700/mo in total debt and still be in a healthy range. This gives him room to take a second loan later if the business grows.
