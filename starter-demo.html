?? Healthy Tax Situation

  • Collecting the right tax rate on every taxable sale
  • Filing on time each month — no penalties
  • Tracking nexus before you owe in new states
  • Separating exempt (B2B) from taxable (retail) sales

?? Dangerous Tax Situation

  • Not collecting tax when you should ? you personally owe it later
  • Missing a filing deadline ? 5–25% penalty on top of what's owed
  • Crossing nexus threshold without registering ? back taxes + fines
  • Example: Miss 3 months of $1,540 filings = $4,620 owed + $1,155 penalty
Nexus means you have enough business activity in a state that the state requires you to collect and remit sales tax there. Originally this meant a physical location (store, warehouse). Since 2018, most states added "economic nexus" — meaning if you sell more than $100K or 200 transactions in that state per year, you must register and collect tax even without a physical presence.

Example: James ships dry-cleaned items to Oregon customers — if those sales hit $100K in a year, he must register in Oregon within 30 days and start collecting Oregon sales tax (0% — Oregon has no sales tax). Different states have different thresholds.
The shortfall means James owes the difference personally:
  • Washington State doesn't care if you "forgot" to charge the customer — you still owe the tax
  • James must pay $985 out of his own pocket to cover the under-collection
  • Going forward: BizScale will auto-calculate the correct tax on every transaction category
  • Fix: Add a line item on receipts showing sales tax separately so customers see what they're paying
How to prevent this: Set each service type (taxable vs exempt) once in BizScale — it auto-applies the correct rate to every transaction and alerts you if a sale is miscategorized.
