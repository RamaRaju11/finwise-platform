Gap Analysis
The 5 gaps between good and world-class

These are not feature requests. They are structural differences between a tool collection and a platform that genuinely changes business outcomes.

1

Manual data entry is the #1 failure point

Users type in estimates. Every calculation is only as good as those numbers.

When a business owner types "Monthly Revenue: $22,000" they are guessing. The real number is in their bank account. Every world-class tool in this space — Nav, Kabbage, Bench, QuickBooks — solved this with live bank connections. Without real data, the platform is helping users make important decisions based on approximations.

🏦
The fix: Plaid or Finicity bank connection. The user links their business bank account once. Revenue, expenses, and cash flow auto-populate everywhere — live and accurate. This single change makes every tool on the platform 10x more useful and trustworthy.

Plaid API Finicity MX Platform
2

A toolbox is not an advisor

Users still have to decide which of 40 tools to open. Great software decides for them.

Right now, a business owner lands on the dashboard and sees a menu of tools. They have to know what their problem is before they can use the platform. World-class software surfaces the problem for them. It watches their data and says: "Your cash runway dropped to 2.1 months. Your loan-to-income ratio crossed 78%. Here are your three priorities this week."

🧠
The fix: A Daily Business Health Engine — a server-side process that runs every night, analyzes each user's actual financial data, and generates a ranked list of their most urgent actions. The dashboard stops being a tool list and becomes a personalized briefing. No user should ever have to decide which tool to open.

Supabase Edge Functions Cron Jobs Rule Engine
3

The lender marketplace is a static list

Showing lender names is table stakes. Live pre-qualification is the business.

The current marketplace shows lender information that a user can find with a Google search. World-class is submitting the user's verified financial profile to lender APIs and returning: "Based on your data, you pre-qualify for $85,000 from Bluevine at 8.4%, $60,000 from OnDeck at 11.2%, and $100,000 SBA 7(a) with a 72% approval probability." This is what Lendio and Fundera built their entire business on.

🤝
The fix: Partner API integrations with 5–10 lenders. SBA, Bluevine, OnDeck, Kabbage/AmEx, Credibly, and Funding Circle all have partner programs. The platform sends a standardized profile, the lenders return real offers. FinWise earns referral revenue on every funded loan. This is also the core monetization engine beyond subscriptions.

SBA Partner API Bluevine API OnDeck Partner Credibly
4

The technical architecture has a ceiling

Static HTML + localStorage cannot support what world-class requires.

This is the most important structural issue. The current approach — static HTML files with data stored in the browser's localStorage — cannot support bank integrations, secure financial data storage, background monitoring, multi-device sync, PDF generation, or AI processing. It works for an MVP. It cannot be the foundation for the best platform in the world.

5

No ongoing relationship with the user

A user sets up their profile, runs a tool, then disappears. Nothing brings them back.

World-class platforms create weekly touchpoints that users actually look forward to. A Monday morning email that says "Your cash runway improved by 0.4 months. You now qualify for 2 new grants with deadlines this month. One action that could save you $1,200 in Q2." is something a business owner forwards to their accountant. That email is also the most powerful retention and upgrade driver possible.

📬
The fix: Weekly Financial Digest emails generated from real user data. Monthly Score Report. Real-time alerts when a threshold is breached (runway below 3 months, loan eligibility changes, grant deadline approaching). Every email is personalised, data-driven, and genuinely useful.

The honest summary

The current platform can take FinWise to a good product that real businesses use and pay for. But the best SMB financial platform in the world requires three things that do not exist yet: a backend that can process real data, live bank connections that replace manual entry, and proactive intelligence that tells each business owner exactly what to do next. Those three things are what separate tools from platforms — and platforms from the best in the world.

The work to get there is significant but it is entirely buildable. Every phase above has a clear starting point. None of it requires waiting for the others.
