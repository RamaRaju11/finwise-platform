🔴 NET LOSS — Business is spending more than it earns after loan payments
1. Income Statement (P&L) — Did the business make money this period? Revenue minus all costs = net profit or loss. Think of it as a scorecard for how well the business performed.

2. Balance Sheet — What does the business own vs owe? Assets (cash, equipment, inventory) vs Liabilities (loans, bills). The difference is equity — what the owner actually "has."

3. Cash Flow Statement — Where did the money actually go? A business can show profit but still run out of cash if it spends on equipment or clients don't pay on time.
Why advisors need all three: Looking at just one statement is like diagnosing a patient with one test. Banks require all three for loan applications. Advisors use all three to catch problems early.
Immediate fixes Sarah should recommend:
  • Review the $10,000 operating expenses — likely $2,000–3,000 can be cut (unused subscriptions, over-staffing)
  • Loan of $42K at current rate costs $1,800/mo — refinance to lower payment or pause non-essential debt
  • At $600/mo loss, the $9,000 cash reserve lasts only 15 months before insolvency
  • Raise class prices by 10% ($14,200 → $15,620) — most yoga students accept small increases
  • Add a membership tier with annual prepayment to boost cash now

Green Leaf Yoga — Auto-Detected Risk Alerts
🔴 CRITICAL: Cash Runway is 0.5 months
At the current burn rate, the business has only 15 days of cash left if revenue stops. Most lenders require 3+ months. Action needed: Immediately reduce expenses or inject $15K emergency capital.
A cash runway under 1 month means: if one big client cancels, one month has a bad week, or an unexpected expense comes up — the business cannot pay rent or staff. This is the most common reason small businesses close.
Example: Green Leaf loses their biggest corporate client ($4,000/mo) in July. With $9K cash, they run out in 2 months.
🔴 CRITICAL: DSCR below 1.0 (currently 0.67)
The business earns $0.67 for every $1 of debt payments owed. This means it technically cannot service its current debt from operating income alone. Action: Refinance the $42K loan to lower monthly payment, or increase revenue by $2,800/mo.
⚠️ WARNING: Operating expenses 30% above industry average
Operating expenses are 56% of revenue vs 43% industry average. Over-spending by ~$2,340/mo. Top targets: review staffing schedule and marketing spend — these are typically 60% of excess costs.

Threshold-based alerts: DSCR drops below 1.0 → alert fires. Cash runway drops under 1 month → alert fires. Expense ratio exceeds industry average by 25% → alert fires. These are the same triggers a CFO would use.
